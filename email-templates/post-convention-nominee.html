It’s been almost 2 weeks since we all gathered together at Highland High — and I have not stopped smiling. I am so excited for your support.

As we gathered for the state convention I want to take a moment to honor the candidates who stood in this race alongside me: Anthony Washburn, Darin Mann, and Jeneanne Lock. Each of them brought real passion, real vision, and a genuine love for this community to this process. District 21 is stronger because they ran. I am proud to call them friends, and I hope they’ll stand with us as we carry this fight forward together.

To every delegate who showed up, listened, and voted — thank you. Your trust is not something I take lightly, and I will carry it with me every single day.

Now — here’s where we are.

Two of us advanced out of convention: myself and Stephen Otterstrom. That means the Democratic primary for Utah House District 21 will be decided on June 23rd — and the delegates, precinct chairs, and vice chairs who came to caucus night are exactly the people who will shape what happens next.

That’s you. And I need you.

There is no Republican in this race. Whoever wins the Democratic primary wins the seat. That means June 23rd isn’t just a primary — it is the election. The stakes couldn’t be higher, and the window to act is short.

I am running to continue the legacy Sandra Hollins has built on the West Side — and to blaze a new trail. That means working across every aisle, every boundary, and every barrier to deliver what our community has always deserved:

🏥 Healthcare on the West Side — our families deserve access, not obstacles.
🏠 Affordability on the West Side — the neighborhood we built should be the one we can still afford to live in.
✨ A West Side that shines — because our communities are vibrant, diverse, and full of extraordinary people who deserve to be seen and heard at the Capitol.
